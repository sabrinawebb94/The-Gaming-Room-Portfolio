# The Gaming Room Portfolio

## Course Information
CS-230 Operating Platforms  
Southern New Hampshire University (SNHU)

## Project Summary
The client for this project was The Gaming Room. They wanted to expand their game, Draw It or Lose It, from Android only into a web-based game that could run on multiple platforms. The goal was to create a design that would support multiple teams, multiple players, unique names, and smooth performance across different operating systems.

## What I Did Well
I think I did well comparing operating platforms and explaining why certain choices would work better for the client. I also did a good job breaking down technical ideas into a way that was easier to understand.

## What Helped During Development
Working through the design document first helped a lot because it gave me a plan before writing code. It made it easier to understand the requirements, think through the structure of the program, and avoid problems later.

## What I Would Revise
If I could revise one part, I would add more detail to security and scalability. Those are both important for a web-based application, especially if the number of users grows over time.

## Understanding the User's Needs
I interpreted the user’s needs by focusing on what The Gaming Room wanted the software to do and what problems they needed solved. This included cross-platform access, unique users and teams, and reliable performance. It is important to understand the user’s needs because the software should solve their problem, not just be technically correct.

## My Design Approach
I approached designing the software by reviewing the requirements first, comparing possible solutions, and then choosing the best option based on performance, cost, and usability. In the future, I would continue using planning documents, diagrams, and testing early in the process to build better software.
